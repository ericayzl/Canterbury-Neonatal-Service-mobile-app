The Road Home

The Neonatal Discharge and Outreach Service is a team of Clinical Nurse Specialists. For some babies, the ‘Road to Home’ can be longer than others, and our goal is to ensure you are comfortable when it is time to take your baby home.

If your baby has continuing medical needs after they leave the Neonatal Unit, such as home oxygen or tube feeding, our Discharge Team will provide all necessary education and supplies. The Outreach team further supports you and your baby at home as needed.

Discharge Planning & Neonatal Outreach

When babies/pēpi born very preterm reach 34-36 weeks corrected gestation or those with complex needs getting ready to be discharged from the hospital, Canterbury Neonatal Service introduces our Discharge Planning nurses to the family. The Discharge Planning nurses work through the discharge needs of each baby with the family/whānau.

The Discharge Facilitators issue a “Road to Home” pamphlet to families. This pamphlet consists of several cue boxes used for discussion with the parents regarding preparation for discharge, home readiness for the baby, and ongoing follow-up. It is a valuable tool as it contributes to a smooth transition home.

Before the discharge date, our service introduces the Outreach Nurses to the family/whānau of the baby/pēpi​​ being discharged from the service. It is important that all support mechanisms and future appointments are in place so that the transition to home goes as smoothly as possible. Once our service discharges the baby to the home/kainga, the Outreach Nurse will visit both baby/pēpi​​ and family/whānau​ for as long as necessary.

Parents are encouraged to spend 24-48 hours living in the hospital with their baby/babies just before discharge. This eases the transfer to home and is an opportunity to consolidate feeding and caring for their baby. Timing will depend on space availability. We have six parent rooms available.

Once stabilised in the hospital environment, our service will discharge babies with Neonatal Abstinence Syndrome into their home/kainga environment. The Outreach Nurses will visit and monitor these babies regularly. This type of approach fosters a more contented baby and family.
