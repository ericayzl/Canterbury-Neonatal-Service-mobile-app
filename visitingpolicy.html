Visiting Policy

Our visiting policy ensures an optimal, safe, and secure environment for your baby by minimising infection risk, protecting your family's privacy, and providing an environment that promotes the baby's well-being and development.

General

Canterbury Neonatal Service recommends no more than three visitors per family at any one time, including parents. Over-stimulation can affect our baby's health and safety, particularly if they are early or unwell. Limiting the number of people at the bedside reduces noise, activity, and infection risk, helping the baby's rest and well-being. If the nursery area becomes too noisy or overcrowded, a nurse may request that you take your visitors out to the parent lounge. Please do not be offended.

Visitors must wash their hands immediately upon entering the unit. Washing hands can prevent the spread of skin infections and cold and flu viruses in the air and on objects such as hands, furniture, baggage, clothing, etc. Sterilising foam is available at your baby's cot.

For Parents

  • Parents are welcome 24 hours per day.
  • Our staff will ask you to wear headphones for privacy reasons. These headphones are available during the medical ward round and nursing handovers.
  • Please do not look or ask about other babies to respect the privacy of other families.

For Visitors

  • No more than three visitors per family at any one time.
  • All visitors are to be accompanied by a parent.
  • Visitors are welcome between 3.00 pm to 8.00 pm.
  • Brothers and sisters of all ages are welcome under adult supervision and are included in the 3-per-family policy.
  • Other children under 12 years of age are not permitted to visit due to the increased risk of infections in this age group.
  • To protect your baby, visitors with a cough, cold, cold sores, rashes, infection, vomiting, or diarrhea should not visit. If you, as parents, are unwell, please discuss this with the nursing staff. We may ask you to see your GP to diagnose or confirm illness before you can return.
  • Non-immunised brothers and sisters of babies should not visit if they have any symptoms of any sort. Any child in contact with other children with Pertussis (Whooping Cough), Mumps or Measles, Chicken Pox, or Shingles should not visit.

For more information about visiting Canterbury Neonatal Services, read the Neonatal Visiting Policy Pamphlet.
